Marginal Disutility of Work: The aggregate adverse effects of work (leisure, health, etc.)
 ^abuPJ3E5

The utility of the wage for a given number of workers is equal to the aggregate adverse effects of work. 

Therefore, if the marginal product of labour is less than the aggregate adverse effects of work 

(the marginal disutility of work), then involuntary unemployment could occur.  ^nPYdo70D

Firms = Demand for Labour 
 ^E5sIa0Nc

Demand Constraint = Supply Constraint  ^tT12l71T

Symbiotic relationship between firm constraints faced by
firms and workers: 

Increasing the constraint on the employment function for
producers allows them to higher more people, this relaxes 
the "rationing constraint" on the household (the other
constraint than the budget limiting consumption). 

Relaxing the rationing constraint allows workers to work
more (assuming that the rationing constraint is smaller 
than the budget constraint). Thus increasing consumption,
incentivising more employment, marginally increasing each
other with each cycle ad infinitum.  ^3N4iB6ha
